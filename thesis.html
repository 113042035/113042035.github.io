1. The Thesis Statement

The bad example is a flat statement of fact wrapped in a cliché; it doesn’t take a stand or give the reader any idea of what specific direction the essay will take. The good example is effective because it is arguable, specific, and acts as a roadmap. The reader knows exactly what the essay will analyze: a reduction in face-to-face interaction and the psychological drive for superficial validation.

Review the examples on the right to see the difference ➔

*** Bad Example:

"Social media has changed how people communicate with each other, and it has a lot of pros and cons."

*** Good Example:

"Although social media allows for instant global communication, it ultimately harms interpersonal relationships by reducing face-to-face interactions and encouraging superficial online validation."
