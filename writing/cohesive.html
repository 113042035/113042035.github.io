2. Cohesive Writing

The bad example reads like a choppy list of bullet points disguised as a paragraph. The reader has to do the mental heavy lifting to connect the thoughts. The good example uses explicit transition phrases ("To achieve this," "which ultimately") and loops back to previous concepts ("this movement goal"). This creates a structural bridge that carries the reader effortlessly from one sentence to the next.

Review the examples on the right to see the difference ➔

*** Bad Example:

"Regular exercise is vital for heart health. People should walk 10,000 steps a day. Sedentary lifestyles cause chronic disease. Doctors recommend moving every hour."

*** Good Example:

"Regular exercise is vital for heart health. To achieve this, doctors recommend walking 10,000 steps a day. Failing to meet this movement goal can lead to a sedentary lifestyle, which ultimately increases the risk of chronic disease."
