3. Coherent Writing

The bad example lacks coherence because it jumps erratically from a benefit (renewable), to a drawback (expense), back to a benefit (lower bills), and then randomly introduces a completely different topic (wind turbines). It has no logical progression. The good example is coherent because it sticks to one singular idea—the renewable nature of solar energy—and unpacks it in a logical, cause-and-effect sequence.

Review the examples on the right to see the difference ➔

*** Bad Example:

"Solar energy is a great alternative to fossil fuels because it is renewable. Furthermore, solar panels can be expensive to install initially. Many homeowners love how it lowers their monthly electricity bills. Wind turbines are also a clean energy source."

*** Good Example:

"Solar energy is a highly effective alternative to fossil fuels because it relies on a renewable source: the sun. Because the sun provides an infinite supply of light, harnessing this power does not deplete Earth's natural resources. Consequently, utilizing solar energy allows society to generate electricity indefinitely without harming the environment."
