2. Cohesive Writing

The bad example is a "summary trap." It simply repeats what the reader already knows from reading the essay, leaving them thinking, “Okay, I knew that. Why did I just read this?” The good example works because it zooms out to the bigger picture. It connects the historical fact (the printing press) to its massive, long-term impact on human history (the democratization of knowledge), giving the essay a resonant and meaningful ending.

Review the examples on the right to see the difference ➔

*** Bad Example:

"In conclusion, that is how the printing press was invented by Johannes Gutenberg in the 15th century and why it changed book production forever."

*** Good Example:

"Ultimately, the invention of the printing press did more than just mechanize book production; it democratized information. By placing knowledge into the hands of the public, it broke the monopoly of the elite and laid the intellectual foundations for the modern, literate world."
